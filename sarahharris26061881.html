Sarah Harris Writes Her Son Ned

Charlottetown
June 26th, 1881
My dear Eddie,

I am only sending a few hurried lines as I have had to write to Mrs. Stone to tell her that I am coming to stay a few days with her. I suppose Sarah must have mentioned to you, her proposal for my going, but I never thought it at all likely that I should go on account of the expense, however Etta wished to go to Halifax to meet Mrs. Robinson on her way from Bermuda, so Tom thought it would be nice for me to go with Etta.

We are to leave here on Wednesday morning & return the following Wednesday; so we can all come home together. I hope Mrs. S. will offer you lodgings too; at any rate you will come over & see me as soon as you arrive in Halifax. Willie received the invitation to the Encaenia illegible today but he cannot go, as he is not ready to leave here until Saturday.

Sarah went up to New London yesterday to stay a fortnight, it will do her good for she was looking thin & pale, she was the prime mover in the plan for my going to Halifax. Frankie is chatting away & whilst I write I have to answer all sorts of questions.

With much love I remain your loving Mother S. Harris

I hope you received your Greek dress all right.

Hilton Hall
